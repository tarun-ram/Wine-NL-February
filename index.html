Dear Wine Cellar Member,

Followers of the New York Times recently got very exercised over a tweet that suggested adding tomatoes to spaghetti carbonara (yes, really). Just as I was pondering this apparent culinary crime, I remembered my own favoured recipe for this classic Roman dish includes a splash of white vermouth, courtesy of domestic goddess, Nigella Lawson. It's because of this unorthodox twist that I've always instinctively reached for a white wine with carbonara but Victoria Moore's piece on matching wine with pasta has tempted me to opt instead for ‘the soft flow of a cerasuolo di Vittoria from Sicily’. What do you drink with yours? Let me know at winecellarexpert@telegraph.co.uk.

Editor’s pick of the week

Il Poggione Rosso di Montalcino 2020, Tuscany, Italy
Victoria Moore describes rosso di Montalcino as being ‘like the young sibling to brunello di Montalcino’, which is good news for anyone looking for a more approachable – and affordable – red to drink with a bowl of pasta. This one is made in a savoury style, with notes of violet, forest floor and distant cherries. Pour it with a tomato-rich dish of rigatoni alla amatriciana though it would also work with the cheesy heft of cacio e pepe.
